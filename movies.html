Kalen's Top 3 Movies of All Time

These are just a few of my favorite movies. I by no means am a movie critic, but I do love good cinema.

  1. Joker (2019)

    2. This movie captivated me with its exceptional cinematography and compelling storytelling. The visual presentation of Gotham City, drenched in gritty realism and stark contrasts, immerses the audience in a world that feels both raw and palpable. The camera work, with its haunting close-ups and evocative framing, brings an intimate connection to Arthur Fleck’s descent into madness. This visual narrative complements the storyline, highlighting the character's isolation and inner turmoil. Every shot feels meticulously crafted, adding layers to the film's atmosphere and enhancing the overall viewing experience.
    The storytelling in "Joker" is another aspect that deeply resonates with me. The film offers a nuanced portrayal of Arthur Fleck, providing a backstory that evokes empathy for a character traditionally seen as a villain. The exploration of societal neglect, mental illness, and the consequences of systemic failure makes the story both poignant and relevant. Watching Arthur’s transformation into the Joker is a heartbreaking journey, as the film delves into the complexities of his psyche and the events that push him over the edge. This character study is both disturbing and enlightening, challenging the audience to reconsider their perceptions of villainy and humanity.
    Moreover, Joaquin Phoenix's performance as the Joker is nothing short of extraordinary. His ability to convey vulnerability, pain, and eventual madness through subtle expressions and intense physicality is mesmerizing. The depth he brings to the character makes Arthur's descent both tragic and believable. This, combined with the film's masterful direction and thought-provoking themes, makes "Joker" a cinematic masterpiece that lingers in my mind long after the credits roll. It's a film that not only entertains but also sparks conversation about important societal issues, making it one of my all-time favorites.



  2. Escape from L.A. (1996)

    3. "Escape from L.A." holds a special place in my heart, largely due to the nostalgia it evokes. Introduced to me by my dad during my childhood, the film became an instant favorite. The character of Snake Plissken, portrayed by Kurt Russell, was the epitome of cool to my younger self. His rugged demeanor, eye patch, and anti-hero persona made him an unforgettable figure in the dystopian landscape of the film. Snake's relentless determination and rebellious spirit captivated me, and I found myself rooting for him at every turn.
    The dystopian setting of "Escape from L.A." adds another layer of fascination. The film's depiction of a post-apocalyptic Los Angeles, with its chaotic streets and lawless zones, creates a thrilling and immersive environment. The creative set designs and special effects, although a product of their time, contribute to the unique charm of the movie. The blend of action, adventure, and a touch of dark humor makes it an exciting ride from start to finish. This world of chaos and survival, coupled with Snake's mission, keeps me on the edge of my seat, no matter how many times I watch it.
    Furthermore, "Escape from L.A." significantly influenced the acclaimed video game series, Metal Gear Solid. The character of Solid Snake, inspired by Snake Plissken, carries forward the legacy of the film in the gaming world. As a fan of both the movie and the video game series, I appreciate this connection and the impact "Escape from L.A." has had on popular culture. The film's enduring appeal, its nostalgic value, and its influence on other media make it a beloved classic in my collection.


  3. The Crow (1994)

    4. "The Crow" is a film that I fell in love with as a kid, and that love has remained unwavering over the years. The movie's storytelling and character development are nothing short of poetic, weaving a beautiful tragedy that resonates deeply. The narrative of Eric Draven, portrayed by Brandon Lee, is a haunting tale of love, loss, and vengeance. The way the film explores themes of justice and redemption through Eric's journey is profoundly moving. Each character, from the tragic hero to the malevolent villains, is meticulously crafted, adding depth and emotion to the story.
    The visual and atmospheric elements of "The Crow" contribute significantly to its impact. The film's gothic aesthetic, with its dark, rain-soaked cityscapes and moody lighting, creates an eerie yet captivating environment. This setting perfectly complements the story's somber and melancholic tone. The cinematography, combined with a powerful soundtrack, enhances the emotional weight of the film, making every scene visually and audibly memorable. The synergy between the film's visual style and its narrative is a testament to its artistic brilliance.
    What makes "The Crow" truly special to me is its poetic nature and the tragic love story at its core. Eric Draven's resurrection to avenge the brutal murder of his fiancée, Shelly, is both heartbreaking and inspiring. The film beautifully captures the depth of Eric's love and his relentless quest for justice, making his character incredibly relatable and sympathetic. Brandon Lee's poignant performance adds another layer of authenticity and emotion, making his portrayal of Eric Draven iconic. "The Crow" is not just a movie; it's a deeply emotional experience that continues to captivate and move me, making it my all-time favorite film.
